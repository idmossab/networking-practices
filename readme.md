# networking-practices

## Module Information

- Repository Name: `networking-practices`
- Module: Building Professional Identity and Digital Presence
- Duration: Maximum 5 weeks

## Why This Module Is Important

In the current tech job market, recruiters spend more time evaluating your LinkedIn and GitHub profiles than your traditional resume. A well-crafted online presence is often the deciding factor in whether you get noticed among hundreds of applicants.

This module equips you with the skills to:

- Create a consistent, professional digital identity across platforms
- Become discoverable by recruiters and hiring managers
- Demonstrate real collaboration through open source and community engagement
- Build lasting professional relationships
- Develop essential soft skills (digital communication, networking etiquette)
- Support your peers, creating a stronger collective network that benefits everyone

Your professional image reflects not only on you but also on your cohort and community. A strong presence builds credibility and opens doors for opportunities.

## Overview & Role Play

You are a soon-to-graduate developer entering a competitive job market. Recruiters are actively searching LinkedIn and GitHub for talent.

Your mission: transform your online profiles into powerful professional assets, contribute to meaningful projects, engage authentically with communities, and network strategically while helping your peers do the same.

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module, you will be able to:

- Build a complete, professional LinkedIn profile optimized for recruiters
- Organize and present projects effectively on GitHub
- Engage with open source projects and demonstrate collaboration
- Establish a visible technical reputation
- Actively participate in professional online communities
- Network effectively at events (campus or external)
- Collaborate with peers through endorsements, recommendations, and resource sharing
- Communicate professionally in digital spaces
- Demonstrate market engagement through interactions with recruiters and industry professionals

## Timeline

- Maximum duration: 5 weeks from start date
- Recommended approach: consistent weekly progress

## Phases & Key Tasks

### Phase 1: Profile Analysis & Research

- Audit current LinkedIn and GitHub profiles
- Research 2-3 exemplary profiles in your field
- Identify best practices in presentation and engagement
- Discover active open source projects aligned with your interests

### Phase 2: LinkedIn Optimization

- Professional, consistent profile photo
- Targeted headline and compelling About section
- Detailed experience, education, and skills sections
- Link to GitHub and portfolio
- Add at least 100 new relevant connections (peers, alumni, recruiters)
- Publish at least 5 posts showcasing projects or new skills learned

### Phase 3: GitHub Enhancement & Community Engagement

- Create a personal GitHub profile README
- Pin at least 2 high-quality projects
- Write clear, professional READMEs and well-commented code
- Follow at least 10 campus peers
- Fork 5+ relevant repositories
- Star/watch 15+ projects
- Follow 15+ active developers in your stack
- Join 3+ developer communities (Discord, Slack, Reddit, DEV.to, etc.)
- Introduce yourself and participate in at least 3 discussions

### Phase 4: Peer Collaboration

- Exchange constructive feedback on profiles with cohort members
- Endorse and recommend peers' skills
- Share useful repositories and resources within the group
- Strengthen collective professional visibility

### Phase 5: Market Engagement

- Interact with recruiters or HR professionals
- Join and participate in relevant LinkedIn groups or discussions
- Share knowledge and insights with your network
- Demonstrate active learning from the broader developer community

### Phase 6: Active Networking (Bonus)

- Attend 1-2 campus networking events
- Participate in external events (hackathons, meetups, conferences)
- Connect with new contacts on LinkedIn within 24 hours

## Tips for Success

- Use the same professional photo across all platforms
- Keep code and documentation clean, readable, and well-structured
- Engage thoughtfully: comment, ask questions, and share learnings
- Start small in communities: introduce yourself and learn before contributing code
- Be authentic rather than promotional
- Follow developers who inspire you to stay current with trends
- Connect promptly after meeting someone in person
